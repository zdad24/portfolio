## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? No.** This is the headline finding: no gradient hero, no Inter, no three-icon feature grid, no side-stripe cards, no glassmorphism. The named DESIGN.md rules were checked one by one and mostly hold: Ink-Is-The-Line ✅, Pixel-Caps-Are-Tiny ✅, Functional-Kicker ✅ (Hero's unnumbered `▸ HELLO_WORLD` vs. real sections' `▸ NN_SECTION` is a genuinely smart distinction), Accent-Ink-For-Text ✅. Two named rules crack under inspection — see Priority Issues.

**Deterministic scan** (`detect.mjs`, exit code 2, 69 findings): the overwhelming majority (60 color + 8 radius = 68 of 69) cluster in `src/tweaks-panel.jsx`, which CLAUDE.md confirms is a **dev-only panel tree-shaken from production** — these are false positives for shipped-experience purposes, though worth a token cleanup pass someday. The one production `layout-transition` warning (`transition: width`) is also in tweaks-panel, same caveat. The `single-font` warning (flagging `index.html` as using only "press start 2p") is a **false positive** — the site demonstrably uses three typefaces (VT323, JetBrains Mono, Press Start 2P); the detector is only reading the `<head>` font declaration order, not the CSS cascade. Real production color hits worth a glance: Tetris piece colors (`#38c8c8` etc. — justified, games need distinct piece hues) and a handful of `rgba(0,0,0,x)` modal/overlay scrims in About/Hero/Projects that aren't yet named tokens — low priority, but "Do use var(--line) etc., never hardcoded hex" is a DESIGN.md rule these technically violate.

**Browser evidence**: no console errors on load, resize, or scroll. One real console *issue* (not error) recurred consistently: 4 contact-form fields (`from`, `name`, `body`, and one more) have no `id`/`name` attributes, relying on `placeholder` alone — this independently confirms and sharpens the design review's contact-form concern into a concrete, reproduced accessibility gap (breaks autofill and screen-reader labeling — this is exactly what persona "Sam" would flag). Spot-checked contrast pairs (body text, links, buttons, form inputs) all cleared 4.5:1+ — but the detector/browser pass did not happen to sample the specific hero status line the design review flagged as opacity-reduced, so that P0 remains a design-review finding not independently re-measured; worth a direct check before shipping a fix.

## Overall Impression

This site clears the bar it's actually trying to clear — nobody mistakes it for a templated AI landing page, and the Hero boot sequence is a legitimate "how did they build this?" moment. The system discipline (theme mechanics staying fixed across Pokémon/Basketball × light/dark, the tactile button contract, the ink-only shadow rule) is real, not incidental. The gap is that the two or three most consequential details for the *recruiter* audience — the one PRODUCT.md says must get the signal "without digging" — are exactly where the site currently under-delivers: a deprioritized status line, a dock that can physically block CTA buttons, and a contact form that will happily report "sent" on an empty message. The biggest opportunity is tightening those specific recruiter-facing moments without touching the parts (Hero, About terminal, theme system) that are already working.

## What's Working

1. **The Hero boot sequence + glitch name-reveal** — timed, purposeful, not gratuitous. The single best moment on the page, and the most shareable.
2. **Theme-mechanics-stay-fixed discipline** — verified live across all four theme×mode combinations: layout, shadow offsets, and border weight never move, only hue/surface tokens re-bind, exactly as DESIGN.md prescribes.
3. **The About terminal's command system** — dozens of hand-written easter eggs, real history navigation, achievement hooks. This is "the medium is the portfolio piece" actually executed, not just claimed.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] The one line recruiters most need is the dimmest text on the page**
Why it matters: `Hero.jsx:311` wraps the "▸ STATUS … open to fall 2026 internships" line in `style={{ opacity: 0.7 }}`. That drops contrast to roughly 2.8:1 against the cream background — under the 4.5:1 AA floor DESIGN.md itself calls out as a watch item. This is the exact fact PRODUCT.md says must surface without digging for the recruiter persona, and right now it's the faintest text in the hero.
Fix: drop the opacity wrapper; if a muted treatment is wanted, use `--ink-mid` (already verified ≥6:1 elsewhere in the system).
Suggested command: `/impeccable audit` (or a direct fix — this is a one-line change)

**[P1] The fixed dock can physically block CTA buttons at common laptop heights**
Why it matters: reproduced live at 1440×900 in both light and dark mode — the `resume.pdf ↗` button in Hero, the source/demo buttons on the last Projects card, and the top of the Experience heading were each fully covered by the opaque dock at natural scroll-stop points. Because the dock correctly uses a solid `--cream-2` background (no-blur rule), it doesn't just crowd visually — it fully blocks clicks until the user scrolls further, with no cue that anything is hidden beneath it.
Fix: add scroll-margin/bottom padding sized to dock height + clearance on sections/CTAs, or give the dock a subtle backdrop-fade so occluded content is at least perceptible.
Suggested command: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Contact form has no field association or validation — confirmed live**
Why it matters: browser console independently reproduced this — 4 form fields (`from`, `name`, `body`, and one more) have no `id`/`name`, only `placeholder`, breaking autofill and screen-reader labeling (persona "Sam" fails here immediately). Separately, the form has no `required` markers or client-side check, so a recruiter can submit an empty `BODY` and see a false "✓ sent" success state.
Fix: add proper `id`/`name`/`htmlFor` association to all four fields, and at minimum a `required` attribute plus inline validation feedback.
Suggested command: `/impeccable harden`

**[P2] Icon-only dock loses its only sighted disambiguation on mobile**
Why it matters: `styles.css:313` sets `.dock-icon::after { display: none }` under 700px, removing tooltips exactly where cryptic glyphs (`DEX`/`ROS`, `EX`, `⌘P`) most need them — touch users can't hover to reveal them anyway, and the `aria-label` is invisible to sighted users. This directly hurts the "first-time visitor unfamiliar with retro-OS metaphors" persona on what's likely majority-mobile traffic.
Fix: give mobile an always-visible short label under (or instead of) the glyph, or a persistent legend/first-visit tooltip sweep.
Suggested command: `/impeccable adapt`

**[P3] Small cracks in the "commit to the bit" tactile system**
Why it matters: About's Quick_cmds buttons (`About.jsx:797-810`) are plain unstyled `<button>`s with zero hover/press feedback, breaking the shadow-collapse contract followed everywhere else — a visible inconsistency an engineer-persona reviewer is likely to notice. Related, smaller: `Projects.jsx`'s `TiltCard` sheen (`mixBlendMode: overlay` + soft radial gradient) is a borderline No-Blur-rule violation worth a second look.
Fix: apply the standard `.btn` treatment to Quick_cmds buttons; reconsider or explicitly grandfather the TiltCard sheen.
Suggested command: `/impeccable polish`

## Persona Red Flags

**Recruiter (fast skim, needs the signal in seconds)**: The one fact that answers "can I hire this person right now" is the dimmest text on the first screen (P0). If they're on a laptop where the dock covers the resume button (P1), the fastest path to the artifact they actually came for disappears with no explanation.

**Sam (accessibility-dependent, screen reader / keyboard-only)**: The contact form's unlabeled fields (P2, confirmed live via console) mean a screen reader announces nothing meaningful for any of the four inputs — this persona cannot reliably complete the one conversion action the whole site points toward.

**First-time visitor (unfamiliar with retro-OS/Pokémon/basketball metaphors)**: Dock glyphs (`⌘P`, `DEX`/`ROS`) assume familiarity with macOS chrome or the theme in-jokes to decode; on mobile with tooltips off (P2), this persona has zero comprehension aid for 7 of 8 nav icons.

## Minor Observations

- Hero's three CTAs (`view projects` / `contact.exe` / `resume.pdf`) are equal-height, slightly diluting the single-primary-action hierarchy `.btn-primary` is trying to establish.
- About's terminal is a fixed 380px tall regardless of viewport, leaving an empty black void below the boot message until a user types.
- Skills stacks language cards plus 3 separate chip grids — comprehensive, but a 5-second-skim recruiter gets a wall of chips rather than a headline read.
- The page's strongest beat (Hero boot/glitch) is in the first 10 seconds; Education → Contact ends quietly on a form and a copyright line, with no callback to the opening bit — a peak-end-rule miss worth considering (not scored as a formal issue since it's a tone choice, not a defect).
- Custom cursor is `cursor: none` globally with no CSS fallback if the `Cursor` component fails to mount — low probability, high severity if it ever does.

## Questions to Consider
